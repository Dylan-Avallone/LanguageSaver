Ser vs. Estar

Definitions

  • Same Word, Different Meanings
  • In English, we use "to be" for everything: But in Spanish, you must choose ser or estar based on the
Explanation
Ser (Permanent or Defining Traits)
  • Identity
    • Who someone or something is:
      Soy Juan. (I am Juan.)
  • Characteristics
    • Inherent qualities or traits:
      Él es alto. (He is tall.)
  • Origin/Nationality
    • Where someone or something is from:
      Somos de México. (We are from Mexico.)
  • Time/Date
    • To express time, days, or dates:
      Hoy es martes. (Today is Tuesday.)
  • Material/Ownership
    • What something is made of or belongs to:
      La mesa es de madera. (The table is made of wood.)
Estar (Temporary or Changing States)
  • Location
    • Where someone or something is physically located:
      Estoy en la casa. (I am at home.)
  • Emotions/Conditions
    • Temporary feelings or states of being:
      Está triste. (He is sad.)
  • Ongoing Actions
    • What is happening right now (present progressive):
      Estoy estudiando. (I am studying.)
  • Results of Actions
    • Temporary conditions due to an action:
      La puerta está abierta. (The door is open.)
